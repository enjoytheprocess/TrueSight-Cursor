About the app

Know what you have. Cook what you'll eat. Donate the rest.

Fridge Chef is the simplest way to know what's in your fridge — and turn it into dinner. Because the food you forgot you bought is the food that ends up in the trash. And the bulk pack you'll never finish? That could feed someone who needs it.

01 —

The problem we keep finding in the back of the fridge

Personal

You don't remember everything you have.

Half-used herbs, leftovers behind the milk, a second bag of spinach you didn't know you bought. About $1,300 per Canadian household, per year — in the trash, because we forget.

Recipients

And for some, the food arrives unfamiliar.

Food bank recipients — often low-income families and newcomers — are handed packages of donated items they didn't choose. Ingredients they don't recognize. Cans they've never used. The food is there. The recipes that turn it into dinner aren't.

Community

And the bulk pack you'll never finish could feed them.

Twelve cans of tomatoes on sale, you'll cook with four. The other eight aren't waste — they're a match for a food bank wish list. The food is sitting right there in your cupboard.

02 —

The solution: a fridge that tells you what's for dinner

Tell Fridge Chef what you have. We suggest recipes that match, and update your inventory when you cook. When you've got more of something than you'll use, we'll let you know who in your community could use it.

03 —

How it works

Add what you have

Tap items into your inventory — quick search, common presets, done. Image recognition arrives in v2.

See your recipes

We surface meals that match what you actually own — ranked by inventory, dietary preferences, and what you can cook.

Cook and update

Tap "I made this" and Fridge Chef deducts the ingredients from your inventory automatically.

06 —

Where the idea came from

Fridge Chef is for anyone with a fridge — but the idea didn't come from nowhere.

It came from working and volunteering with non-profits — food banks among them — where recipients receive packages of donated items they didn't choose and don't always know how to cook. Cans they don't recognize. Ingredients they've never used. Food that ends up in the trash — not from lack of need, but from lack of know-how.

That's the gap Fridge Chef was first built to close. Once the matching engine worked for that, we realized it could help the rest of us, too — but the heart of the project still lives in those organizations and the people they serve.

07 —

One promise

Fridge Chef works the same on the phone in your hand and the laptop on your counter. The kitchen is where you are — the app meets you there.
